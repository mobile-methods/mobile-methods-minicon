What are mobile methods?

This mini-conference convenes invited experts and OSU scholars to advance research on mobile technologies and the methods used to study them. The conference is rooted in Communication and welcomes allied social science perspectives. The Minicon is meant to offer a venue to integrate ideas and innovations tied to the use of mobile methods. This scope includes digital traces (mobile sensing, screenshot data) and self-report (experience sampling method, ecological momentary assessment) collected through smartphones and wearable devices – along with the linkage of these two branches of ecological measurement.

Event overview

This event will include panels focusing on the following questions and topics:

  • What are mobile methods?
  • Measurement issues in mobile methods
  • Linkage of mobile sensors and surveys
  • Where should mobile methods go next?

Why mobile methods matter

Smartphones structure how people communicate, coordinate, and engage with institutions. Mobile methods help researchers examine these dynamics while also improving how behavioral and communication data are collected and interpreted.

Conference scope

The Mini-con focuses on both substantive questions about mobile technology use and methodological questions about mobile-generated data. Communication serves as the center of gravity, with openness to fields such as Psychology and other social sciences.

Focus areas

Communication-centered inquiry

Centering Communication theory and empirical work while creating space for cross-disciplinary collaboration.

Cross-disciplinary social science

Relevant for scholars in Communication, Psychology, and related fields studying social behavior in mobile contexts.

Mobile data and analytics

Topics include smartphone-based data, communication patterns, app use, and geolocated use in daily life.
